--- layout: publication year: 2022 title: "Neo: Generalizing Confusion Matrix Visualization to Hierarchical and Multi-Output Labels" link: https://github.com/apple/ml-hierarchical-confusion-matrix awards: - Best Paper Award authors: - Jochen GÃ¶rtler - Fred Hohman - Dominik Moritz - Kanit Wongsuphasawat - Donghao Ren - Rahul Nair - Marc Kirchner - Kayur Patel doi: 10.1145/3491102.3501823 arxiv: "2110.12536" type: - Conference venue: CHI venue_location: New Orleans, LA highlight: false venue_tags: - CHI venue_url: https://chi2022.acm.org/ tags: - Data Science pdf: https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.12536 --- The confusion matrix, a ubiquitous visualization for helping people evaluate machine learning models, is a tabular layout that compares predicted class labels against actual class labels over all data instances. We conduct formative research with machine learning practitioners at Apple and find that conventional confusion matrices do not support more complex data-structures found in modern-day applications, such as hierarchical and multi-output labels. To express such variations of confusion matrices, we design an algebra that models confusion matrices as probability distributions. Based on this algebra, we develop Neo, a visual analytics system that enables practitioners to flexibly author and interact with hierarchical and multi-output confusion matrices, visualize derived metrics, renormalize confusions, and share matrix specifications. Finally, we demonstrate Neo's utility with three model evaluation scenarios that help people better understand model performance and reveal hidden confusions.
